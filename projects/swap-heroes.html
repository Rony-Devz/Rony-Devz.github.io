Swap Heroes: Eternal Legends

Glaive Games · formerly “Solaria: Dawn of Heroes”

A live, real-time squad-based ARPG where players collect heroes and swap between them mid-battle. As the game designer on a tiny core team — just the two founders and me — I work hands-on across the systems that make a live-service RPG tick: combat and bosses, game modes, progression and economy, with a focus on midcore depth that stays approachable.

The game designer on a three-person core team (the founders and me) — hands-on across heroes, combat, enemies & bosses, plus game modes, economy and UX. I've grown from junior into a broad role over three years.

Systems & Progression

Depth that unfolds over time

A squad ARPG lives or dies on its long game. I work across the progression that gives players a reason to keep investing — hero upgrade paths and the pacing that decides how power and new content open up over time. The guiding idea was the thesis I bring to everything: real depth for players who want it, without walling off newcomers who just want to jump in and play.

Combat & Moment-to-Moment

Swap-based, readable, satisfying

The core hook is swapping between heroes mid-battle, so combat has to read clearly and feel good second to second. I work on hero kits, enemy and boss patterns, and difficulty tuning — chasing fights that are easy to follow on the surface but reward timing, team-building and smart swaps underneath.

Economy & Monetization

A healthy, honest economy

A free-to-play economy only works if players trust it. I contribute to the currencies, sinks and sources, and the gacha hero collection — how players earn, spend and progress, and how monetization stays fair rather than pushy. (Specifics are under NDA; this is the public-facing framing.)

Live-Ops & Game Modes

Reasons to come back

A live game needs a steady drumbeat of things to do. I work on game modes and the live-ops cadence that keep players engaged week to week — content designed to support retention and a healthy economy, not just short-term spikes.

How I approach it

Data-informed, player-first

Coming from a VC-analyst background, I lean on data and competitive research as much as instinct — studying how comparable games solve the same problems, then designing for our players. Three years on a live title have taught me to ship, watch how real players respond, and keep iterating.
