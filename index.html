My take

Accessible depth

I believe the future of mobile is core/midcore depth — rich mechanics and systems — delivered with the low floor and broad appeal of casual games. Depth earns retention; approachability earns the audience. My job is to make them the same design — from the moment-to-moment feel up through the meta layers that keep players invested.

Low floor

First-session clarity and momentum — anyone can start and feel good fast.

High ceiling

Meta systems that keep rewarding mastery, planning and experimentation.

Great to touch

Moment-to-moment gameplay that feels responsive, readable and satisfying.

Data-informed

Decisions backed by metrics, competitive research and honest teardown.

About

A designer with an analyst's eye

I'm a Data-Driven Game Designer for Mobile F2P — pairing casual-game approachability with long-term midcore depth.

I'm a game designer focused on mobile live-service games, and it comes down to one thing: making games that are fun to play — and stay fun. The way I get there is accessible depth — core and midcore systems delivered with the low floor and broad appeal of a casual game, so newcomers aren't scared off and committed players always have somewhere deeper to go. For the last three years I've put that into practice as the designer on a small live-ARPG team — the two founders (CEO & CPO) and me — growing from junior into a broad, hands-on role.

My path here wasn't the usual one. I served as an officer in the Israeli Navy, where I learned to lead, stay disciplined and execute under pressure, then spent a year and a half as an analyst at VGames, a games-focused VC, working on investment and competitive market research. That mix taught me to pair design instinct with data and market context — and to take products apart to understand what actually makes them work.
